Drink: Coffee

Ethiopia is the world's seventh largest producer of coffee, and Africa's top producer.

The major markets for Ethiopian coffee are the EU (about half of exports), East Asia (about a quarter) and North America.

Harar, Yirgacheffe and Limu are regional varieties that are trademarked names with the rights owned by Ethiopia.

Sidamo, Genika and other regional varieties also available.

Source: wikipedia.org

Food: Injera with Curry

Injera is a sour fermented flatbread with a slightly spongy texture, traditionally made out of tiny, iron-rich teff seed flour.

It is the national dish for Ethiopia. It is the most important component of any Ethiopian or Eritrean meal. It is often both the serving platter and utensil for a meal.

Source: wikipedia.org

Habesha Clothing

Habesha clothing is the traditional attire of Ethiopian people.

These clothings are usually worn by Ethiopian and Eritrean people at formal events, holidays and invitations.

It comes in many forms nowadays. It is made of cotton fabric, and typically comes in white, grey or beige shades. Many women also wrap a shawl called a netela around the formal dress.

Source: wikipedia.com

Oromo Clothing

Traditional Oromo clothes consist of woya for men, which are toga-like robes, usually white, and a skirt called a wandabo for women. Oromo women also wear qollo and sadetta, cotton cloths traditionally hand-spun and hand-woven, and sometimes other garments are worn such as leather or animal skin robes.

These clothings are usually worn in Oromiya regions of Ethiopia at formal events, holidays and invitations.

Source: journals.sagepub.com

Source: ethiopedia.blogspot.com

Sport: Abebe Bikila

Shambel Abebe Bikila, (August 7, 1932 - October 25, 1973) was an Oromo Ethiopian marathon runner who was a back-to-back Olympic marathon champion. He is the first black African Olympic gold medalist, winning his first gold medal at the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome while running barefoot. At the 1964 Tokyo Olympics, he won his second gold medal.

Source: Wikipedia.orgs

Sof Omar Caves

Sof Omar Cave is the longest cave in Ethiopia at 15.1 kilometres (9.4 mi) long. When surveyed in 1972, it was the longest cave in Africa.

It is situated to the east of Robe, in the Bale Zone of the Oromia Region in southeastern Ethiopia, through which the Weyib River (Gestro River) flows.

Source: wikipedia.org

Lalibela Rock Church

The Rock-hewn Churches of Lalibela are monolithic churches located in the Western Ethiopian Highlands near the town of Lalibela, named after the late-12th and early-13th century King Gebre Mesqel Lalibela of the Zagwe Dynasty, who commissioned the massive building project of 11 rock-hewn churches to recreate the holy city of Jerusalem in his own kingdom. The altitude is around 2,480m.

Source: wikipedia.com

Bale Mountains

The Bale Mountains in the Oromia Region of southeast Ethiopia, south of the Awash River, are part of the Ethiopian Highlands.

They include Tullu Demtu, the second-highest mountain in Ethiopia (4377 meters), and Mount Batu (4307 meters).

The Bale Mountains are home to many of Ethiopia's endemic animals, notably the Ethiopian wolf (Canis simensis), found on the Sanetti Plateau. The park also contains the Harenna Forest, situated to the south of the mountains, which is a largely unexplored area.

Source: wikipedia.org

Ertalle

Erta Ale volcano is a large basaltic shield volcano in the Erta Ale volcanic range in the central northern Danakil depression (NE Ethiopia).

Erta Ale is only 613 m high, but as typical for a shield volcano has a very gentle slopes and a large 40 km diameter base.

Source: volcanodiscovery.com
